================================================================================
UNDER — GAME VISION & DESIGN DOCUMENT
================================================================================

This document defines the unit structure, weapons, and gameplay parameters
for a WWII tactical RTS game where players control between 50 and 800 units.

The design is based on historical doctrine but simplified for gameplay.

================================================================================
GAMEPLAY SCALE
================================================================================

Typical map sizes:

| Battle Size     | Units per side | Example                |
|-----------------|---------------|------------------------|
| Small skirmish  | 50–120        | Reinforced platoon     |
| Medium battle   | 120–300       | Company                |
| Large battle    | 300–800       | Battalion              |

================================================================================
UNIT HIERARCHY
================================================================================

Division (~15,000)
└── Regiment (~3,000)
    └── Battalion (~700–900)
        └── Company (~120–180)
            └── Platoon (~35–45)
                └── Squad (~8–12)

Players usually command:
• 1–4 Companies
• or 1 Battalion

================================================================================
GERMAN ARMY (WEHRMACHT 1940)
================================================================================

Doctrine Summary
----------------
German infantry doctrine centered around the machine gun.
The MG34 provided most of the squad's firepower while riflemen supported it.

Strengths:
• High suppression capability
• Excellent machine guns
• Good combined arms coordination

Weakness:
• Fewer rifles per squad
• Less armor protection in early war tanks

German Infantry Squad (Gruppe) — 10 soldiers
---------------------------------------------
1 Squad Leader (MP38/MP40)
1 MG34 Gunner
1 MG Assistant
1 Ammo Carrier
6 Riflemen (Kar98k)

Role: Machine gun forms the base of fire while riflemen maneuver.

FRENCH ARMY (1940)
================================================================================

Doctrine Summary
----------------
French infantry relied more on rifle fire and defensive tactics.
Their tanks were often stronger individually, but poorly coordinated.

Strengths:
• Strong individual tanks (Somua S35, Char B1)

Weakness:
• Slower operational mobility
• Less flexible command structure

================================================================================
WEAPONS BALANCE — DETAILED
================================================================================

Design Principles
-----------------
- Bolt-action rifles are accurate and reliable at medium range.
- Submachine guns dominate at very short range.
- Light machine guns are the backbone of squad firepower.
- Heavy machine guns lock down lanes and open ground.
- Mortars are for suppression, smoke, and indirect fire.
- Anti-tank guns are lethal against early-war armor when positioned well.
- Tank guns should feel powerful, but line of sight, reload, and turret
  limitations matter.

Range Model
-----------
Suggested conversion: 1 game meter = 5 real meters

Combat bands:
- Point blank: 0-20 m
- Close: 20-80 m
- Medium: 80-250 m
- Long: 250-600 m
- Extreme: 600 m+

Note: Weapons should not simply stop working past effective range.
Accuracy, suppression, and penetration should fall off by band.

ARMOR AND PENETRATION TABLE
================================================================================

Rules of Interpretation
-----------------------
Penetration >= Armor + 15:
  Very high pen chance. High damage. Possible one-shot kill.

Penetration >= Armor:
  Good pen chance. Normal damage. Crew shock likely.

Penetration within 10 below armor:
  Partial / inconsistent pen. Spall, stun, module damage possible.
  Better from closer range or flank.

Penetration > 10 below armor:
  Usually no penetration. Minor shock only. Use flanks or stronger weapons.

Angle modifiers:
  Front armor: listed value
  Side armor: listed value
  Rear armor: listed value
  30° obliquity: effective armor +10%
  45° obliquity: effective armor +20%

================================================================================
SIDE AND REAR VULNERABILITY
================================================================================

Side shots:  +20 penetration bonus equivalent
Rear shots:  +35 penetration bonus equivalent
Point-blank flank shot: additional +10 penetration equivalent
Moving target hit penalty offsets some bonus.

This means:
- Pak 36 can threaten Somua or heavy French tanks from the flank
- 25mm Hotchkiss reliably kills Panzer II and threatens Panzer III from side
- Autocannons become dangerous against armored cars and rear armor

================================================================================
DAMAGE RESULTS ON PENETRATION
================================================================================

When penetration occurs, choose from:
- Crew stunned
- Gun disabled
- Engine damaged
- Track damaged
- Turret jammed
- Vehicle knocked out
- Catastrophic kill

Light gun penetrations often disable before destroying.
Medium/high penetration hits should have high knockout chance.

Examples:
- 25mm into Panzer II side: high chance of kill
- Pak 36 into H35 front: moderate chance of damage only
- 47mm SA 35 into Panzer III front: high chance of knockout
- Pak 36 into Char B1 front: effectively none

================================================================================
BALANCE RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

Infantry Balance:
- German infantry should win frontal open-ground engagements if MG34 can deploy.
- French infantry should be resilient in cover and stronger in raw rifle count.

Armor Balance:
- German armor should feel more numerous and responsive.
- French armor should feel tougher and more dangerous individually.

Support Balance:
- Mortars must punish static MG nests.
- Anti-tank guns must dominate exposed tanks.
- Recon should be essential for spotting AT guns and mortar positions.

Do not let German tanks beat French tanks in pure frontal duels too easily.
German success should come from:
  - flanking
  - AT gun ambushes
  - artillery
  - coordinated multi-unit attacks

French tanks feel individually superior, offset by:
  - slower reaction
  - higher cost
  - fewer units
  - command/control inefficiency

This creates asymmetry and makes both sides interesting.
